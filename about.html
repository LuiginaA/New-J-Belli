What is a Chiropractor?

A chiropractor is a licensed healthcare professional specializing in diagnosing, treating, and preventing biomechanical disorders of the neuromusculoskeletal system, which includes the bones, joints, nerves, muscles, tendons, and ligaments. In South Africa, chiropractors complete six years of rigorous study to earn their “Doctor” title.

Chiropractic care focuses on the body’s natural ability to heal itself. Through manual adjustments, chiropractors restore proper joint alignment, helping to manage pain and improve overall body function. These adjustments can enhance the performance of various systems throughout the body.

Chiropractors address a wide range of conditions and offer a holistic, drug-free approach to health. They serve as an alternative or complement to conventional medicine, aiming to reduce reliance on medications and avoid surgery whenever possible.

What Does a Chiropractor Do?

Chiropractic treatment is safe, non-invasive, drug-free, and holistic. Chiropractors are highly trained in spinal manipulation, using their hands or specialised instruments to apply controlled, gentle force to joints. This improves joint motion, blood flow, nutrient delivery, and overall function.

Chiropractic care is commonly used for:

  • Headaches and migraines
  • Neck and lower back pain
  • Shoulder, hip, knee, and ankle pain

Chiropractic supports a healthy lifestyle for people of all ages, from babies to the elderly.

Chiropractors do not prescribe medication. Instead, they assist the body's own healing processes.

Additional therapies may include:

  • Relaxation techniques
  • Muscle stimulation
  • Heat or ice therapy
  • Stretching and exercise
  • Activity and lifestyle modification

Practical suggestions to adjust daily habits, reduce discomfort and support recovery.

What is an Adjustment?

An adjustment is a specialised chiropractic technique involving a gentle, precise movement applied to a joint. The spine consists of vertebrae separated by shock-absorbing discs and supported by facet joints, which allow movement and stability.

When joint motion becomes restricted, surrounding tissues may become inflamed, irritating nearby nerves and causing pain in areas such as the neck, arms, back, hips, or legs.

During an adjustment, a clicking or popping sound may be heard. This is caused by gas release within the joint capsule â€” a natural and harmless process called cavitation.

Adjustments are safe and effective for restoring joint motion in the spine and extremities, including the shoulders, hips, and knees.
